      What are the Benefits of Having a Good Case Fan?       

      Different computer components such as the RAM, graphics cards, and processors have enhanced power consumption and speed. This has contributed to the high amount of heat that the system produces. If such components are not cooled down, they may not perform their best. The motherboard can throttle as it tries to bring the temperatures back to normal levels. 

      Having a case fan is vital since this can eliminate the excess heat from the case. You should invest in a good case fan since this can help you enjoy your computers components for a long time and save you money in the long run. Though you can still buy the 120mm case fan, this is not the best for pushing lots of air out. Choosing the best case fan, such as the 140mm, can help dissipate the heat faster to keep the inner components safe from damage. 

      If you are into video editing, 3D animation, or gaming, you should choose a good case fan that has a size of 140mm. Since these case fans have larger blades, they are efficient and circulate a lot of air at lower speeds. You can also enjoy a silent operation from a good case fan.

      How to Choose the Best 140mm Case Fan? (Buying Guide)

      Case fans have been evolving over the years. In 2021, they are no longer plastic frames that surround fans. The modern case fans are a complete package. Finding the best 140mm case fan from the numerous options available can be a challenge. Considering the following crucial factors can help you narrow down your search.

      Fan speed

      Checking the speed of a case fan is important since it can show how well it performs. The size of the case fan determines the speed. This is often measured in RPM (revolutions per minute). You should go for a large case fan since this can run at a lower RPM. It can also circulate a lot of air and operate more quietly compared to a small case fan.

      Number of fans

      Since a computer uses multiple fans, you can save money by getting a pack of fans. The best 140mm case fans come as a pack that contains up to 5 fans. Choosing such can help you get great value for your cash. Most fan packs are also easy to install in the computer case.

      Noise levels

      You should also confirm how loud a case fan operates before paying for one. The noise levels of a 140mm case fan are computed in decibels (dB). This shows how loud the fan can get when it is running at the maximum RPM. The noise levels of most 140mm case fans range between 10 and 35 dB. 

      Airflow

      In such case fans, the airflow is measured in CFM (Cubic feet per every minute). This indicates the amount of air that the case fan is able to push per minute. As you compare the best 140 mm case fans, it is advisable to choose one that has a high CFM. Do not forget to consider things such as the speed and its size.

      Fan lighting

      Like most computer peripherals, case fans come with different lighting options. If you want to improve your PCs aesthetics, you should check the lighting options that a case fan comes with. While some of them include RGB lighting, others have a single color LED. Consider a case fan that comes with inbuilt lighting so that your computer can look better.

      Power connection type

      Before choosing a 140mm case fan, you should find out how it connects to the system. Most of the case fans of this size use the 4-pin connection type. This can easily connect to the 4-pin connector on a motherboard. If you choose one that uses the three-pin connection type, you can still connect it to the 4-pin connector on the motherboard. This, however, uses only three pins. 

      The market also offers other case fans that use the connection type known as Molex. Rather than connecting to the motherboard, such a case fan can connect to the power source directly. Apart from that, some of the case fans have a combination of two connection types, such as 3-pin and Molex. 

      Bearing type

      140 mm case fans come in different bearing types. The most common types include ball, hydrodynamic, and sleeve. If you want the best case fan, choose one that uses the hydrodynamic bearing. This is a self-lubricating type of bearing that is considered to the most durable. Though the ball bearing can be a bit loud, it can also last longer than the sleeve bearing.

      Conclusion

      Whether you wish to upgrade your PC or minimize its components pressure, you should consider investing in a 140mm case fan. This can keep the components cool and optimize performance. Start comparing the best 140mm case fans that we have reviewed above and choose an ideal one. Do not forget to check the crucial factors we have also discussed. All the best!
