As you play League of Legends, you\u2019ll hear various terms like AP Damage, Attack Damage, Burst Damage, DPS, etc. It is enough to leave any new player wondering and questioning \u2013 Just what the hell does all of this mean? Well, as always, we\u2019ve got you covered. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

In this article, our focus is on DPS. But don\u2019t worry, we\u2019ll cover the other ones eventually. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

DPS stands for Damage per second. <\/em>This is a term used to describe how much Damage a Champion deals over a period of time. DPS is usually used in relation to a longer<\/em> duration rather than a short and quick one. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

This means that low<\/em> DPS Champions are those that have long delays between their damage outputs. For example, a Champion like Lux can deal massive Damage with her infamous spells, especially her Ultimate. But, when these are exhausted, Lux must retreat from the frontline to wait for her cooldowns to expire. This creates a gap in her Damage per second<\/em>, meaning that she\u2019ll deal less than high DPS Champions over a longer period. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

But what is high DPS<\/em>? Well, let\u2019s take Jinx, for example. Jinx is an ADC, meaning she hits hard and she hits fast. Jinx also has good buffs in her kit that give her good attack speed boosts, allowing her to dish out Damage even faster. In the same duration as Lux, Jinx will just keep hammering the enemy. She deals continuous and large Damage during a longer period, i.e., she deals higher Damage <\/em>per<\/em><\/strong> <\/strong>second. <\/em><\/p>\n\n\n\n

So, to put it into perspective: A low DPS Champion has to wait<\/em> a significant amount of time to deal Damage, while a high DPS Champion uses continuous attacks and Damage to stay on top during the entire fight. <\/p>\n\n\n\n
