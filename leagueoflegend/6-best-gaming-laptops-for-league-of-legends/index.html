      6 Best Gaming Laptops for League of Legends

      November 16, 2020, 23:18

      Multiplayer Online Battle Arena games (MOBAs) aren’t really resource-intensive. Therefore, it would be okay to play these games on a mid-range notebook that is running on a standard NVIDIA GTX GPU and an i5 processor – this is enough to play a game like League of Legends, and it would have decent frame rates. 

      However, gaming is a process where aspirations, ambitions, and preferences change as time goes by. If you enjoy gaming and play League of Legends, there may be many other games you’re interested in playing. Games including Dying Light, Apex, Fornite, and much more. These types of games require a high-end gaming computer. 

      Nearly every gaming developer lists the minimum specifications required to run their game. Whether it’s an MMORPG, MOBA, Battle Royale, or a basic MMO game, regardless of the game’s name, it’s your levels of indulgence, game-playing expertise, and frequency of gaming that will determine the type of laptop you should get.

      Today, I am here to introduce you to the best laptops for League of Legends, but before I do that, let’s see the minimum and recommended system requirements to run League of Legends.

      Minimum and Recommended System Requirements to Run League of Legends

      Minimum System Requirements

      • CPU 8th – Gen Intel Core i5
      • RAM – 8GB
      • Storage – 256GB SSD
      • Display – 15.6-inch HD IPS (1366×768
      • Graphics – 2GB NVIDIA GeForce MX150
      • Battery – Up to 4 Hours

      Recommended System Requirements

      • CPU – 8th Gen Intel Core i7 or Up
      • RAM – 16GB
      • Storage – 512GB SSD or Up
      • Display – 15.6-inch IPS type FHD (1920 x 1080)
      • Graphics – 4GB+NVIDIA GeForce RTX or GTX
      • Battery – Up to 6 hours

      Take note that the performance of a laptop isn’t just restricted to the GPU. The CPU handles most of the simulations, but it’s the RAM that you really have to pay attention to. This is where the game files from your storage device can be found. The display’s quality will determine the visual quality as well, so you will also want to pay close attention to this. Basically, what you need is a balanced machine capable of handling all of your gaming needs. 

      So, without further ado, here’s the list of the 5 best gaming laptops for League of Legends :

      1) MSI GE75 Raider – The Best For All Kinds Of Gaming

      The large screen on this laptop caught our attention in the beginning because, you know, there’s nothing quite like playing League of Legends on a large screen. Sure, you can always hook up a second screen to the laptop (that’s what we do), but a laptop with a large screen is almost a must-have. 

      For those of you that are more than just a seasoned gamer, the MSI GE75 Raider Laptop should do the trick. With this laptop, you can spend hours upon hours indulging in League of Legends. 

      Processing Performance

      • The processing performance is something you’re going to like, thanks to the Intel Core i7-10750H CPU. The existing processor features a total of 12MB worth of cache memory with a maximum turbo clocking speed of 5.0GHz. This makes it so that the chipset can efficiently process gaming logic. 

      NVIDIA GeForce RTX 2060 GPU

      • This laptop has a powerful NVIDIA GeForce RTX 2060 GPU in it, which will allow you to achieve frame rates close to 1000 FPS for games like PUBG – running at 1080p on max texture rendering level.

      16GB RAM

      • The 16GB RAM comes in handy as well – it makes it easier to run different MOBA games, yes, including League of Legends. It’s also enough if you plan on live streaming and need to run the live streaming software while playing the game. With this laptop, you can stream your gameplay on platforms like YouTube Gaming and Twitch without having to worry about lag.

      Storage

      • The storage has a 512GB SSD and a 1TB HD. You can use the big HDD to store your project files and favorite movies that you don’t need access to on a regular basis. You should install the most demanding games on the SSD, as this will help minimize the loading times.

      Display Screen

      • With this laptop, you will have a 17.3-inch Full HD display screen, which is a dream come true for most gamers. The 144Hz refresh rate paired with the 3ms overdrive response time is what makes it possible for you to play every mid-range and low-end game out there (on the highest settings) without putting a damper on the frame rates.

      Keyboard

      • The Steel Series keyboard has backlighting support, making it so that you can see the keyboard in the dark. Plus, MSI makes sure you get that nice anti-ghosting key compatibility that works in tandem with the touchpad. 

      Speakers

      • This laptop creates a perfect gaming atmosphere with its two big speakers. So, if you don’t plan on using the headphones and prefer using loud speakers, you’re going to like this feature.

      Weight

      • This laptop features a thermal design, but it’s still pretty thin. Of course, it’s not like the ultrabooks, but that is okay with us. This laptop weighs 5.75 pounds, so it’s easy to carry around.

      Battery Life

      • The battery life should last up to 4 hours, and this is with continuous usage. 

      Pros

      • Powerful processor
      • Big screen
      • Innovative keyboard
      • Dual storage unit

      Cons

      • There is no Thunderbolt 3 Port

      Conclusion

      To sum it up, the MSI GE75 Raider would be great for League of Legends players, even professionals. It can also run games such as PUBG, Apex, World of Warcraft, Fortnite, and Ark: Survival Evolved.

      Ports

      Next on our list of the best laptops for League of Legends, we have the Legion Y540 that comes to us from Lenovo. Surely you guys have heard of Lenovo before, right?
