      What Defines a Control Mage in League of Legends?

      by Davis
      November 22, 2021

      Mages are champions in League of Legends that use and depend on their abilities to do damage instead of their basic attacks. A typical example of a mage is Annie or Ahri. Mages do magic damage and are often chosen as the mid-laner or support. Its rare to see a mage in the top-lane, although some players have started to use mages in the jungle, such as Ecko. Mages use their abilities to charm, stun, slow and damage enemies. This makes them ideal for playing as an assassin in the mid-lane or support in the bot-lane.

      What is a control mage? As the name indicates, these are mages that can control the battlefield either by using crowd control or area of effect damage. Some mages have abilities that, when used on one enemy, trap and bounce off nearby enemy champions as well. This makes it difficult for the enemy champions to regroup, strategize and attack your team. Often a mage such as Brand can single-handedly paralyze enemy movements and kill over half the enemy team.

      Different players will give a different opinion of what a control mage is, and Riot has so far not made a formal category of Control Mage. The most obvious examples are Ziggs, Brand, Lux, Syndra, etc. These champions can control the battlefield at a safe distance while engaging multiple enemies simultaneously. This frees up space and time for your team to maneuver and combine their attacks and increases the chances of winning team fights. Viktor, Oriana, Azir, Karthus, Anivia, and Lisandra can also be characterized as control mages.

      All of the above seems to indicate that control mages really are masters of the battlefield, and theres no getting around them. However, they are not invincible, and they do have certain weaknesses that can be exploited. If you have a champion such as Zac, you can close down the distance between you and the enemy control mage. If an enemy champion is right in your face, there isnt much you can do with a control mage.

      Similarly, Tryndamere can waltz in rather quickly, and your control mage will be in trouble. The trick is to always have a tank around your control mage to protect against such gimmicks by the enemy side. This way, your team can profit off the most from having a control mage in the team.
