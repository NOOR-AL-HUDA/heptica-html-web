      5 Best Mid Lane Champions in League of Legends Patch 10.15

      by Davis
      July 28, 2020

      Even though mid lane hasn’t received any major changes in this Patch, you can probably see some new champions on it. So, without further ado, here are the 5 best mid lane champions in League of Legends Patch 10.15

      5. Nocturne

      Nocturne is one of the strongest sleepers picks in the mid lane this patch because he does really well against most picks, including Zed, TF, and Yasuo. He has an all-around kit with a damage boost, spell shield and an attack speed buff. His Ultimate also allows for some nasty roams as he can gank any lane from the River, instantly helping him to catch an early kill. Nocturne is definitely worth picking up as a pocket pick as he’s shown to always come back in a variety of metas.

      4. Talon

      Talon is a great pick because of his early kill potential and his ability to roam using Assassin’s Path (E). Many meta mid lane champions (Cassiopeia, Kassadin, Galio) need a few items before they reach their full potential, but Talon is different. Talon is the optimal counter pick to most of those champions because he can apply immense pressure early in the lane and either collect solo kills or push the wave and look to roam. A fed Talon forces the enemy team to play conservatively because of his roaming potential and makes it easier for his team to get leads that they can snowball into a victory.

      3. Fizz

      Fizz is an absolute monster in the mid lane. It doesn’t matter if you’re a new or experienced player because playing against him is a nightmare. His ability to move through units, while also being Untargetable ensures that he can avoid impact burst, before punishing opponents. Fizz has been top tier for about 7 Patches now and League of Legends developers simply do not care about his state right now. No changes are being made to him and he still continues to be the very best in the mid lane.

      2. Galio

      Galio is the complete crowd control package in the mid lane. He can burst down backline targets while also providing a ton of crowd control. Galio’s best asset in solo queue, however, is his ability to roam. His ultimate, Hero’s Entrance (R), allows him to turn trades for his allies around the map and gives him incredible mobility. Galio is a great choice for the mid lane for his ability to turn team fights in his favour and get his allies ahead.

      1. Zed

      With the new love of AP Junglers, AD Assassins like Zed can finally be played without the imbalance of damage split. Zed has made his way back to being number one in the mid lane. His strong laning phase and ability to dominate one-on-ones makes him an excellent choice. However, Zed is a hard champ to master and requires a lot of practice before you can fully dominate your opponents.

      Do you like more of AD Assassins or AP Mages? Let us know in the comments below.
