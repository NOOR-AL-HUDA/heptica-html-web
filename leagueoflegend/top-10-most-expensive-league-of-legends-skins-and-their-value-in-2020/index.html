      Top 10 Most Expensive League of Legends Skins and Their Value in 2020

      June 7, 2020, 22:19

      If you started playing League of Legends in its early stages, you’ll probably recognize some of these skins, but do you know their value in 2020? Even though the skins on the list down below don’t really look good, they’re so valuable thanks for their rarity.

      10 Most Expensive League Skins

      10. Pumpkinhead Fiddlesticks – 50$

      If you’ve ever wanted to play with the Fiddlesticks character while rocking a Sleepy Hollow kind of aesthetic – a specialty skin only ever made available in 2010 during one of the original special LOL holiday events – you’ll want to track down this pumpkin-headed version.

      While you won’t look precisely like the headless horseman (the pumpkin head is squarely attached to the top of Fiddlesticks with this skin (you are going to look pretty haunting. The design wasn’t initially all that beloved by fans – in fact, most of them thought it was pretty lackluster – but now it is one of the truly rare League of Legends skins that gets you instantly identified as an OG account holder.

      9. Rusty Blitzcrank – 70$

      It’s almost impossible to think of right now, but 11 years ago (give or take a couple of months) you could actually walk into your favorite gaming store or electronics store and purchase a physical copy of League of Legends – something new players won’t even be able to imagine.

      But OG players on League are going to remember this well, especially since they were able to land a completely custom skin – this one – decked out in full silver plated armor that’s impossible to get any longer.

      You’ll have to pony up a decent chunk of change to land this skin, that’s for sure.

      8. Championship Riven (2012) – 130$

      Every single account holder that attended the World Championship event back in Season Two (which
      feels like a lifetime ago, doesn’t it?) was immediately gifted this wild Riven skin.

      The next couple of months or absolutely filled with games and lobbies of players rocking this skin
      variation, and the secondary market went bananas in the immediate aftermath of the WC with this skin
      regularly commanding prices close to $400 (and sometimes even higher than that).

      Things have come back down to a little bit for what used to be one of the rarest LoL skins ever made.
      That’s because the folks at RIOTS decided to re-release the skin during the 2015 World Championships –
      but only one person was able to actually claim this prize by picking the winner of every tournament.

      7. Silver Kayle – 140$

      It’s almost impossible to think of right now, but 11 years ago (give or take a couple of months), you could actually walk into your favorite gaming store or electronics store and purchase a physical copy of League of Legends – something new players won’t even be able to imagine.

      But OG players on League are going to remember this well, especially since they were able to land a completely custom skin – this one – decked out in full silver-plated armor that’s impossible to get any longer.

      You’ll have to pony up a decent chunk of change to land this skin, that’s for sure.

      6.Victorious Jarvan IV – 160$

      If you played League back in 2011 and were hypercompetitive, the chances are pretty good that you dedicated yourself to dominating the original rated season that took up most of that year.

      Players that successfully completed this initial challenge were given this skin as a gift, starting a tradition that would later follow on for pretty much every season of League to follow. This was the original Victorious Skin, though, and there’s something really cool about having it on your account – particularly when you want to flex on new players that are trying to get the latest Victorious skin.

      A lot of people consider this to still be the best looking of all the Victorious options, even though there were no new animations added to this unique skin. The silver and gold pallet is absolutely gorgeous, though, and it’s still a skin that a lot of top-tier players like to use if they have the chance to do so.
